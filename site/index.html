Zero Trust

Zero Trust is a security model based on the "never trust, always verify" principle. This means that all users, devices, and applications must be verified before they are granted access to the network. Zero Trust is not a product that can be purchased but rather a strategy for securing solutions. However, some products, such as Azure, can be used to implement Zero Trust. {% include feature_row id="feature_row_zero_trust" %}

Breaches

Data breaches and hacks can cause severe financial losses, damage to reputation, and legal liabilities for companies. Individuals can experience identity theft, financial losses, and damage to credit scores. It can also cause emotional distress and a loss of trust in companies that handle personal information.
